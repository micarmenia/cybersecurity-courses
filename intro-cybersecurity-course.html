About This Course

This prep course is designed for anyone curious about cybersecurity. Whether you're exploring a potential career change, looking to protect yourself online, or just fascinated by how hackers work, this course gives you the essential foundation you need.

Over 24 hours of hands-on learning, you'll discover how cyber threats actually work—not just in theory, but through practical exercises and real-world scenarios. You'll learn the mindset of both attackers and defenders, giving you a complete picture of the cybersecurity landscape.

Who Should Take This Course

This course is perfect for:

  • Complete beginners with curiosity about cybersecurity
  • Students exploring tech career paths
  • Professionals looking to understand security fundamentals
  • Anyone who wants to protect their personal devices and accounts
  • Tech enthusiasts ready to learn something new

Prerequisites

You just need basic computer knowledge—how to use a browser, install software, and navigate files. If you know some programming basics, that's helpful but not required. We'll teach you everything else from the ground up.

What You'll Learn

By the end of this course, you'll have practical knowledge in:

  • Understanding how malware actually works and spreads
  • Recognizing phishing attempts and social engineering tactics
  • Using Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) techniques
  • Securing your personal devices and online accounts
  • Identifying common vulnerabilities in systems
  • Understanding web application attacks (file uploads, XSS, SQL injection)
  • Defending infrastructure from common threats
  • Participating in Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions

What You Can Do After

After completing this course, you'll be able to:

  • Decide if cybersecurity is the right career path for you
  • Progress to our Advanced Cybersecurity course
  • Better protect yourself and your organization from cyber threats
  • Understand security news and reports you read online
  • Start participating in beginner-level CTF competitions
  • Have informed conversations about security with IT teams
  • Apply basic security principles in your daily digital life
